In 2014, ConvertKit was making $1,500/month. I was exhausted and ready to shut it down.
Today, we just crossed $35M in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR).

The turning point wasn't a magic feature or a viral growth hack.
It was direct sales. I stopped waiting for people to find my blog posts and started doing the unscalable work. I began doing free concierge migrations for top-tier bloggers, manually moving thousands of subscribers from Mailchimp to ConvertKit myself.

If your SaaS is stuck at a few thousand MRR, stop tweaking the landing page. Pick up the phone. DM your ideal customers. Do the unscalable work until it hurts.

Why we distribute profits to our team instead of chasing a massive exit.

Most tech companies treat their employees as stepping stones to a liquidity event. 
We operate differently at ConvertKit. We are 100% independent and we plan to stay that way. Instead of the promise of a future buyout, we distribute profit shares twice a year. 

This aligns incentives. When the company wins, the team wins today, not 10 years from now. 
Profitability is the ultimate freedom in software.

**Content:**
The "Creator Economy" is broken. 
Platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram own the distribution, which means they own the creator. If the algorithm changes, your business dies.

The only true asset a creator has is their email list. 
An email list is an audience you own entirely. Nobody can restrict your reach, and nobody can take it away from you. 

Build your following on rented land, but always, always drive them to owned land.

**Content:**
A framework I use for building software businesses: The Ladders of Wealth Creation.
Most people try to jump straight to building a massive SaaS company. That’s like trying to lift 500 lbs on your first day at the gym.

The progression looks like this:
Level 1: Trade time for money (Freelancing / consulting)
Level 2: Productize a service (Design Joy)
Level 3: Digital Products (E-books, courses, templates)
Level 4: Software as a Service (ConvertKit)

Start at Level 1, learn the industry deeply, and use the cash flow to build your way to Level 4.

**Content:**
Transparency is our biggest marketing channel. 
Since day one, ConvertKit has published our revenue dashboard publicly. Anyone can see our MRR, churn rate, and customer LTV. 

Why? Because trust is the currency of the internet. By showing our highs and our lows, we didn't just get users, we built advocates. 

If you're an indie hacker or startup founder, consider opening up the hood. Build in public. It’s terrifying, but the ROI is unquantifiable.
