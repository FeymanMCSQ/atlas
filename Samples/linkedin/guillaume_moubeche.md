How we scaled lemlist from $0 to $10M ARR in 3.5 years without spending a dime on ads.

1. B2B communities. We built "The lemlist family" before the product was even perfect.
2. Cold Outreach. We drank our own champagne and used our tool to pitch early adopters.
3. Content Marketing. We didn't just write generic SEO blogs. We shared our actual cold email templates that got 80% open rates.

## Post 3
**Likes:** 18,500
**Content:**
The single biggest mistake SaaS founders make when pricing their product:
They price based on what it costs them to run the servers.

Wrong. You need to price based on the VALUE you deliver. 
If your software saves an SDR 10 hours a week, and an SDR makes $60/hr... your software is generating $2,400 a month in value. Don't charge $15/month for it.

We doubled our pricing at lemlist in the early days. Conversion rates didn't drop. Revenue doubled overnight. 
Value your product, and your customers will too.

**Content:**
I used to think that to be a great CEO, I had to work 80 hours a week and micromanage everything.
I was wrong. That's how you build a job, not a company.

The real job of a CEO:
1. Set the vision.
2. Hire people who are smarter than you.
3. Make sure there is money in the bank.
4. Get out of their way.

If you are still approving every tweet and reviewing every pull request, you are the bottleneck in your own startup.

"Build it and they will come" is the biggest lie in software.
I see brilliant engineers build beautiful, technically perfect SaaS products every day. And they launch to crickets. 

Why? Because they spent 6 months coding and 0 hours talking to users.
Before we wrote a single line of code for lemlist, I pitched the concept to 100 agency owners. 

Sell the vision first. Write the code second.
