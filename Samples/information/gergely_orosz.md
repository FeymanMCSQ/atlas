# Gergely Orosz (The Pragmatic Engineer)
**Platform:** LinkedIn / Newsletter
**Style:** Deeply analytical, Engineering-First, Insider Perspective
**Vibe:** Pragmatic, realistic, anti-hype.

## Key Principles:
1. **The Hook:** Directly addresses a major industry change or engineering controversy using plain language. 
2. **The "Meat":** Backs up claims with actual data, insider quotes, and software engineering realities. 
3. **The Utility:** Explain the *systemic* "why". (e.g., Why did the cloud outage happen? Why are salaries dropping?)
4. **The Tone:** Calm, objective, authoritative. Strips away all marketing fluff.

## Example Post:
The recent data showing AI coding assistants yield "0% net productivity gains" shouldn't surprise anyone who has worked in a large-scale enterprise system.

Coding has never been the bottleneck. 

Here is what the data actually reveals:
- Junior engineers are producing 2x more code.
- Senior engineers are spending 30% more time reviewing and debugging that code.
- Deployment cycle times remain flat because architectural tech debt is increasing.

When you use an LLM, you are essentially generating boilerplate at light speed. But a SaaS business doesn't survive on boilerplate. It survives on maintainability, state management, and strict business logic constraints.

AI hasn’t solved the engineering bottleneck; it has merely shifted the friction from the "creation" phase to the "verification" phase. It shifts the burden from the junior developer to the senior reviewer.

If your team is deploying Copilot, you don't need more code. You need stricter automated testing pipelines to handle the sheer volume of code being produced. 

Are your senior engineers feeling the review tax?
